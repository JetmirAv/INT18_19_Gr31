F l i g h t s

Economy

Whether you call it coach, standard, main cabin, or "cattle class", economy is the most basic class. The seats here are the narrowest, ranging from 16 inches to just over 19 inches wide and the seat pitch,the distance from one seat to the seat in front or behind.

  • No baggage included
  • No drink included
  • No food included

Premium Economy

Premium economy refers to the "better" seats on an aircraft on domestic flights, it also offer additional perks like better food,a greater baggage allowance and amenity kit.

  • Baggage max. 15kg
  • Drink (Tea/Coffee)
  • Full meal service

Business

The happy medium between coach class and first class is business class. The experience ranges from a wider seat that reclines back but doesen't lie flat, full meal service, and an amenity kit, to a seat that converts to a full lie-flat seat,personal minibar,multi-course meals and a full bar area .

  • Baggage max. 23kg
  • Full lie-flat seat
  • Personal minibar
  • Multi course-meals

First class

Passengers can't get upgraded any further than first class, which is a luxurious experience that varies depending on the airline. The experience of first class ranges to lie-flat seat with a massage feature, in-flight entertainment and full meal service. The residence with living room, bedroom and private bathroom.

  • Baggage max. 32kg
  • Seat with massage
  • Private bathroom
  • Full meal service

Bus travel between Europe’s big cities can be a cheap way of getting from A to B, but it’s rarely the quickest way to
travel and is often subject to last-minute delays and cancellations. Trains are a fast, comfortable alternative to budget bus travel, and with a Eurail Pass you can even enjoy unlimited travel across Europe using just one ticket at a fixed price. A bus ticket
